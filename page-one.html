Introduction to the History of Sewing

The art of sewing is at least 20,000 years old. Ancient peoples joined pieces of material us-ing bone and horn needles and animal sinew for thread. Around the 14th century iron nee-dles were invented, and by the 15th century there were eyed needles. Later sewing needles were made of steel, as they still are today. In the 1800s the first practical sewing machine appeared, and today most sewing is done by machine, though some sewers still choose to perform fine sewing and finishing by hand.
