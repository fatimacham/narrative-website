How To…


Thread a Needle

Cut the thread with sharp scissors to prevent frayed ends. Stiffen the thread using water, then proceed to push the end of the thread through the hole aka the eye of the needle. If you still have difficulty, use a needle threader tool.

Tie a Knot

Though this skill may seem obvious, a tip we recommend is to use your index finger to wrap the thread around, then proceed to pull the end of the thread through the loop created.

Running Stitch

This is the most basic stitch, done by running the needle and thread up and down the cloth at a regular distance.
(Click for more instruction)

Backstitch

This stitch is a strong sturdy stitch resembling machine stitching in appearance, much stronger than a simple running stitch.
(Click here for instructions)

Sew a Button (4-hole)

Firstly, thread the needle, then mark the chosen spot. Next, push the needle through the back of the button and thread though each of the holes diagonally a few times. Finally, secure and cut the thread.

Hemstitch

Also known as a hemming stitch, it is used to hold a hem in place. Depending on the stitch you use, it can be invisible or show on the outside of fabric.
